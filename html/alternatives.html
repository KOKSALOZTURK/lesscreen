    Toddler Activities (Ages 1-3)

    Sensory Play

    • Fill a bin with dry rice or beans for pouring and scooping
    • Use a muffin tin to sort small objects like buttons or pom-poms
    • Fill a container with water and add plastic animals for a "safari" experience
    • Use a large cardboard box as a playhouse or car
    • Fill a bin with shredded paper and hide small toys for a treasure hunt
    • Make a "nature bin" with leaves, rocks, and twigs for exploration
    • Create rainbow spaghetti with food coloring for tactile exploration
    • Fill a baby bathtub with water and ice cubes for pouring experiments
    • Make sensory bags with different textures rotated weekly
    • Pour rice between containers in a contained space

    Fine Motor Skills

    • Use a hole punch to create holes in paper and string yarn through
    • Use a small container to practice pouring and scooping
    • Stack plastic cups or blocks to build towers
    • Use tongs to transfer small objects from one bowl to another
    • Sort buttons or beads by color or size
    • Use a hole punch to create holes in paper and string yarn through
    • Fill and empty containers with water using cups or spoons
    • Use a spray bottle to water plants or clean surfaces
    • Poke colorful pom-poms through bottle openings
    • Thread Cheerios onto upright spaghetti stuck in clay
    • Arrange clothespins on cardboard edges
    • Stick paper pieces to contact paper shapes on walls

    Creative Expression

    • Use crayons or washable markers to color on paper
    • Use washable markers to draw on paper or cardboard
    • Make collages with magazine cutouts and glue
    • Use finger paints or washable paints to create art
    • Make homemade playdough and shape it into objects
    • Use stickers to decorate paper or cardboard
    • Make a "nature collage" with leaves, flowers, and twigs
    • Cover toys with painter's tape for an "unmasking" activity
    • Decorate pet rocks and create homes for them
    • Draw pictures for family members
    • Sit in a box and decorate the inside with washable markers

    Practical Life Skills

    • Practice pouring water from one container to another
    • Use a sponge to wipe surfaces or clean toys
    • Help with simple cooking tasks like stirring or mixing
    • Sort laundry by color or type
    • Wash toys in a baby bathtub and dry them on a mat
    • Use sponges with soapy water to "clean" kitchen cabinets
    • Match pairs of socks
    • Float and ladle toys in water
    • Fill a small tub with water and wash plastic toys
    • Use a spray bottle to water plants or clean surfaces
    • Practice folding washcloths or napkins
    • Use a small broom and dustpan to sweep up crumbs

    Tips for Parents

    Dear parents! Remember that the best activities often involve simple materials and lots of imagination. These alternatives to screen time help develop critical thinking, creativity, and independence while creating joyful childhood memories!

    • Rotate activities to maintain interest
    • Keep a "boredom buster" jar with activity ideas on slips of paper
    • Set up activity stations that can be used independently
    • Encourage children to create their own list of screen-free activities
    • Involve children in planning and preparing activities
    • Limit screen time to specific hours of the day
    • Be a role model by engaging in screen-free activities yourself
    • Encourage outdoor play and exploration
    • Provide opportunities for social interaction with peers
    • Encourage creativity and problem-solving through open-ended play
    • Be patient and flexible; not every activity will be a hit
    • Use everyday items creatively (e.g., cardboard boxes, kitchen utensils)
    • Encourage children to take breaks from screens and engage in physical activity
    • Incorporate educational elements into play (e.g., counting, sorting)
    • Encourage children to explore their interests and hobbies
    • Provide a variety of materials for creative expression (e.g., art supplies, building blocks)
    • Encourage children to play with siblings or friends to foster social skills
    • Set aside time for family activities that everyone can enjoy together
    • Encourage children to use their imagination and creativity in play
    • Be open to trying new activities and experiences
    • Encourage children to take breaks from screens and engage in physical activity
